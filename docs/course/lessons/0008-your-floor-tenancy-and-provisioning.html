Your floor: tenancy & provisioning

How the shop builds each customer an isolated floor of the switchboard — brick by idempotent brick — and why nobody can wander onto someone else's.

⏱ ~12 minutes🎯 Goal: explain structural tenancy and resume-anywhere provisioning from memory🧩 Builds on: Lessons 1, 7

Picture a hotel that builds a whole floor for every new guest. The crew keeps a construction diary: every time a brick is finished, they write it down and put the pencil away. If the power cuts out mid-wall, nobody panics — tomorrow they open the diary, see "wall three: done", and continue from brick four. Never from scratch, never a duplicate wall.

And the room keys? Your floor number isn't assigned — it's computed from your name. The front desk can always re-derive it, so it can never be lost; and since no two guests have the same name, no two guests can ever compute the same floor. You never say "floor 12, please" — you show your ID, and the desk works out which floor is yours. That hotel is exactly how the shop (IAP phone_service, Lesson 7) builds your tenant on the Wazo switchboard.

The build, as a picture

The shop's phone_service.pbx_tenant walks a linear state machine — 11 forward steps, then ready:§7

planned → tenant_created → incall_context_created → outcall_context_created
→ internal_context_created → outcall_context_attached_to_internal_context
→ trunk_sip_endpoint_created → trunk_created → trunk_attached_to_sip_endpoint
→ outcall_created → trunk_bound → ready

Plus three side exits: ready ↔ disabled (billing suspension — the floor exists, the door is locked), released (floor demolished), and failed when a tenant sits stuck past the provisioning TTL — 2 hours at HEAD (PROVISIONING_TTL; older docs still say 24 h).pbx_tenant.py:42,741

The last brick deserves a second look: phase 4 registers one Wazo webhookd subscription for the whole tenant — call created/answered/ended, push and push-cancel, voicemail events — pointing back at the shop, authenticated by a freshly minted per-tenant wazo_webhook_secret. The doorbell wire is part of the building, not an afterthought: a tenant is not ready until the switchboard can talk back._step_create_webhook_subscriptions

Lay every brick twice, safely

Every step follows the same discipline, and it's the diary from the hook:

  • Lookup before create. Each _step_* first asks Wazo "does this already exist?" and only creates what's missing. Running a step twice converges; it never duplicates.
  • Commit after every success. _advance_until_blocked commits the transaction after each step, so a mid-chain Wazo outage strands the tenant at its last good state, not at zero. The invariant: state == S ⟹ every Wazo object up to step S exists.pbx_tenant.py:75-80
  • Resume from anywhere. A cron retries stuck tenants every 5 minutes; the build is also kicked eagerly the moment you order your first number (_place_orders), and lazily by any PBX call that arrives while the floor is unfinished.phone_service_number.py:287
Every brick can be laid twice safely. Idempotent steps + commit-per-step + a dumb retry cron is the whole resilience story. No rollback logic, no "undo half a floor" — the saga only ever moves forward or re-lays the same brick.

The big idea: nobody names their floor

Here is the design decision this lesson exists for. The tenant is a pure function of the authenticated client: wazo_tenant_uuid = UUID(hex=client_id). The customer database never sends a tenant id — it just authenticates, and the shop derives which floor is theirs. You can't ask for someone else's floor, because you never ask for a floor at all.§7

The shop enforces this at its own boundary too. Every shop→Wazo request goes through wazo_api.pbx_request, which demands exactly one of tenant_uuid=… or master_scoped=True. Declare neither, or both, and you get a hard ValueError at the call site — not a silent write into the master tenant. And the derived Wazo-Tenant header is written after merging caller headers, so nothing a caller supplies can override the scope.wazo_api.py:121-126

Cross-tenant access is impossible by construction, not by ACL diligence. There is no permission an admin could misconfigure, no token a customer could leak, no header an attacker could inject. The isolation is structural: wrong tenant ids simply cannot be expressed.

It wasn't always like this. The old design gave each tenant an ACL policy, an API user, and scoped Wazo tokens that were handed to the customer's database. All of it is deleted. Today customers hold zero Wazo credentials — the only thing that ever touches Wazo directly is the browser's SIP/WSS media leg (Lesson 1), because audio can't transit a JSON API.§7

The gate on every operation

Every /pbx/<op> route passes through one gate, _require_active_tenant, before anything runs:pbx_controller.py:154-178

SituationAnswerMeaning
No tenant and no orderable number403You never engaged the PBX. Go buy a number first.
Tenant exists but is still building503Kick the provisioning cron, tell the client to retry. Its next attempt eventually succeeds.
Tenant ready (or disabled)runThe op executes, tenant-scoped.
503 means "come back in a minute", not "broken". While the floor is under construction, every PBX call both nudges the build forward and politely refuses. The client's ordinary retry loop is the waiting room — no special "provisioning pending" protocol exists, and none is needed.
The 401-vs-404 trap. Wazo answers 401 — not 404 — for an unknown Wazo-Tenant. So a bare 401 is ambiguous: expired credential, or a floor that vanished behind our back? On 401 the shop probes tenant existence with an explicit master-scoped check: confirmed gone → re-plan the tenant and answer 503 (the rebuild self-heals, the retry succeeds); still there → surface a real auth error (502). Never rebuild on a bare 401 — or a transient auth blip would bulldoze and rebuild customer floors._reprovision_if_tenant_gone · pbx_controller.py:181-204

Healing on the Odoo side (a preview of Lesson 11)

The shop heals its floor; the customer database heals its occupants with the same philosophy — level-triggered, not event-triggered. A checklist (_filter_under_provisioned) defines what a fully provisioned user looks like: SIP credentials, PBX ids, wakeup token, incall ids. Events merely trigger checks; a 30-minute heal cron plus a 60-second post-failure retrigger re-drive whatever is missing.voip_did_number.py:501-541

And when ids were lost mid-crash (Wazo objects created, Odoo transaction rolled back), the retry hits a create-conflict — and adopts the orphan: it finds the Wazo object by its deterministic name, re-owns it, and re-issues its secrets. Deterministic naming is the same trick as the deterministic tenant UUID, one level down.

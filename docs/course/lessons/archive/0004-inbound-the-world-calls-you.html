The world calls you

How a call from any phone on Earth finds your browser and makes it ring — and quietly drops a call card in your Odoo at the same time.

⏱ ~10 minutes🎯 Trace an inbound call + explain DID routing↩ the mirror image of Lesson 3
Start simple 🧒

A big hotel has one public phone number on its sign, but hundreds of rooms inside. When you call that number:

  1. The phone company delivers your call to the hotel's front desk.
  2. The front desk has a little list: "calls to our main number → ring room 12." It rings room 12.
  3. And it jots in the guest log: "a call came in for room 12 at 3pm."

That little list — "public number → which room rings" — is the one new idea in this lesson. In phone-system words it's called the incall.

The journey, as a picture

Zoom in on the PBX (Lesson 1 reminder). "Wazo PBX" is still two programs: Kamailio (the edge) receives that inbound INVITE off the trunk, and Asterisk (the engine) does the incall lookup and rings you. Same split as outbound — just the door it comes in.

The one new idea: the incall (DID → destination)

The whole trick of inbound is a routing rule that answers "a call arrived for this public number — whose desk should ring?"

Inbound is the mirror of outbound

You already learned outbound in Lesson 3. Inbound is the same picture flipped — which makes it easy to remember:

The payoff: one inbound call lights up all three planes

Remember the planes from Lesson 1? A single incoming call uses all three at once — this is the moment they click together:

◆ SignalingThe INVITEs and 200 OK that ring your browser and set the call up.
● MediaThe actual voice — RTP/SRTP bridged PSTN ↔ trunk ↔ your browser.
⬢ ConfigThe webhook to Odoo that makes a call card appear in your history.

The ring and the call-card are different lanes: your browser ringing is signaling; the call appearing in Odoo is a config-plane webhook fired in parallel. That's why your phone can ring even if Odoo is briefly slow — and why a call can show in history slightly after it rings.

Why you must be "registered" (Lesson 2 callback). The incall points at extension 1001 — but Asterisk rings 1001 wherever it last saw a REGISTER from you. No registration (browser closed, not logged in) → there's nowhere to send the INVITE → it falls through to voicemail or a fallback. Registration is what makes your desk reachable.

🔌 Where this lives in our code. Two separate wires, both set up by the enterprise side:
  • The incall route — when a DID is assigned to a user, voip.did.number._sync_pbx_incall() creates the "DID → extension/queue" route on the PBX, remembered as pbx_incall_id / pbx_incall_extension_id. That's the front desk's little list, written by Odoo.
  • The call card — the inbound webhook lands at POST /voip/pbx/call_event, and voip.call get-or-creates the record (state = calling) so it shows in your log.
(onboarding §8 provisioning & §9 inbound)

Extra niceties on the browser side (real polish, not core): if you have several tabs open only one plays the ringtone (the "ringleader"), a background tab can ring via a service-worker notification, and Do Not Disturb silences everything. Worth knowing they exist; we'll not go deep here.

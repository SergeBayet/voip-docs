Following the money

A nameless receipt arrives after every call. Twice-bridged, once-charged, never doubled — here's how.

⏱ ~12 minutes🎯 Goal: trace one call.cost event from Telnyx to a cost line, naming every invariant🧩 Builds on: Lessons 7, 9

Picture the shop one last time. It buys phone service wholesale from the phone company (Telnyx) and bills you retail (IAP credits, with a markup). But the phone company's receipts are maddening: they arrive after you hang up, they carry no name — no "from", no "to", just a cost — and if the mail truck hiccups, the same receipt shows up twice. A 12-year-old running this shop would double-charge someone by Friday. This lesson is how Parrot's shop never does: every receipt finds its owner, lands exactly once on the customer's tab, and never gets lost — even when everything retries.

One call becomes one charge

Walk it hop by hop, in the receipts language:

  1. The shop keeps a guest book. During the call, Telnyx sends lifecycle events (call.initiated/bridged/answered/hangup), Ed25519-signature-verified. They are never forwarded — each just records a phone_service.telnyx.call row mapping call_control_id → client + from/to/direction. The client is found via the DID (your number is from on outgoing, to on incoming). Rows are garbage-collected after 12 hours.§12
  2. The anonymous receipt arrives. After hangup, the sip-trunking call.cost event lands — no parties, no client, just a cost keyed by call_control_id. The shop joins it to the guest book. If no client is resolvable yet, it answers 503 — Telnyx redelivers later. No local retry queue was built; the carrier already has one.
  3. Retail pricing. Only sip-trunking cost parts are customer-billable (_CUSTOMER_BILLABLE_CALL_PARTS); anything else is absorbed by Odoo. Each kept part is multiplied by the markup ICP (floored at 1.0) and the total rounded to 4 decimals. Zero after filtering ⇒ swallowed end-to-end: not forwarded, not charged.
  4. Charge exactly once. One phone_service.webhook.event row per event_id (UNIQUE + savepoint) gates everything: only a fresh event forwards the HMAC-signed payload and calls payment._reserve(client, "call_cost", event_id, …) — itself protected by UNIQUE(reference_type, reference_id). A redelivered receipt hits the unique key and does nothing.
  5. The capture always lands. Call costs are post-paid: the IAP capture passes allow_negative_balance=True, so it succeeds even into a negative balance, flagging the payment overdrawn. The minutes were already consumed — refusing wouldn't undo the call.
  6. The enterprise's copy. The forwarded event hits /voip/api/event (HMAC-verified), which dedupes by event_id and creates a voip.call.cost.line storing the marked-up credits — the customer's price, never Telnyx's.

The identifier gap, bridged twice

Big idea: nobody in this pipeline shares an id, so the id gets rebuilt twice. The Telnyx billing leg, the Wazo call your PBX handled, and the Odoo call record are three worlds that never exchanged a common key. The shop bridges gap one deterministically; the enterprise bridges gap two heuristically — and both are deliberate designs, not bugs.

Bridge 1 — shop side (exact)

  • Anonymous call.cost ↔ guest book, joined on call_control_id
  • Works because Telnyx stamps its own id on both the lifecycle events and the cost event
  • Failure mode: correlation row missing ⇒ 503 ⇒ Telnyx redelivers within the 12 h window

Bridge 2 — enterprise side (best-effort)

  • Cost line ↔ voip.call: direction + last-9-digit number match + time window
  • Multiple candidates ⇒ closest end-time wins (with a warning log)
  • No match ⇒ orphan; a 30-min cron re-matches orphans up to 24 h old

Why can't bridge 2 be exact? Telnyx bills its own leg of the call, identified by call_control_id. The call your enterprise recorded is the Wazo/SIP conversation — born on the PBX, with its own SIP identifiers. Nothing in the SIP exchange carries the Telnyx id back, and nothing in the cost event carries the SIP one forward. So the enterprise matches on the only shared physics: the same two numbers talked in the same direction at about the same time (occurred_at − billed seconds − 5 min … occurred_at + 1 min). The orphan cron exists mostly for one race: the cost webhook can land before the call record even exists.§12

When the charge fails: retries and the bouncer

A capture can fail (IAP down, transient errors). The recover-payments cron (every 10 min) retries pending payments older than 10 min and failed ones whose next_attempt_at has passed, with exponential backoff: 30 min × 2tries, capped at 1 day. At ≥ 5 tries the client counts as delinquent. Enforcement is a separate robot: the reconcile cron (every minute) recomputes the "disabled" intent from the live IAP balance — falling back to the overdrawn/delinquent snapshots when IAP is unreachable — and drives reality to match: Telnyx connection detached (inbound goes dead), tenant trunk unbound, numbers suspended. Re-enabling is symmetric: balance back ≥ 0 flips suspended → active. Numbers still suspended on the 25th are released. Monthly number fees ride the same payment ledger; a rotation cursor spreads that cron's load across clients.§12,§15

Design taste worth copying: nowhere in this pipeline is there a home-grown retry queue for webhooks. Failure ⇒ non-2xx ⇒ the sender redelivers; unique keys absorb the replays. The retry machinery you don't build is the retry machinery you never debug.

The money invariants

InvariantEnforced by
"Charged to the client" ⇒ "charged to us by Telnyx"Ordering: Telnyx DELETE before local released; final month billed before a drift-release№.py
One payment per receipt, everUNIQUE(reference_type, reference_id) on phone_service.payment
One event row per hopUNIQUE(event_id) on phone_service.webhook.event and on voip.call.cost.line
Zero-cost events never surfaceDropped after markup filtering — not forwarded, not charged, no line

Two enterprise-side finishing touches: voip.call.cost is simply the sum of its cost lines, cost_display renders empty (not "0.0000 Credits") when zero, and every cost field is visible to VoIP admins only. And when a customer genuinely runs out of credits at purchase time, the shop's error arrives as a stable insufficient_credits key that the enterprise maps to a translated message — never the server's raw English text.

The SIP call lifecycle

What actually travels on the signaling lane — from "I'm online" to "goodbye" — in plain words first, then the real SIP names.

⏱ ~9 minutes🎯 Goal: trace a call hop-by-hop and name every message🧩 Builds on: Lesson 2 (two planes)
Start simple 🧒

Imagine you want to talk to your friend Sara through a hotel with a front desk operator. Three things have to happen, in order:

  1. You tell the front desk "I'm in room 12, send my calls here."
  2. You ask "please connect me to Sara" — her phone rings — she picks up — you both say "ok!"
  3. You talk. When you're done, someone says "bye" and the line drops.

That's a phone call. SIP is just the polite back-and-forth that arranges steps 1, 2 and the goodbye. The actual talking in step 3 is the media plane from Lesson 2 — a different lane entirely.

Now the real words 🧑‍💼

Each plain sentence above has an official SIP name. Same conversation, professional vocabulary — this is the language your colleagues will actually use:

Plain wordsSIP messageLane
"I'm online — ring me here"REGISTERsignaling
"Connect me to Sara (here are my codecs)"INVITE + SDP offersignaling
"Her phone is ringing"180 Ringingsignaling
"She picked up (here's my codec)"200 OK + SDP answersignaling
"Great, got it — we're connected"ACKsignaling
the actual talkingRTP / SRTPmedia
"Bye" (hang up)BYE (or CANCEL before answer)signaling

Two ideas worth pocketing:

  • SDP is the codec handshake. The INVITE carries an offer ("I can speak Opus or G.711, send audio to this IP:port"); the 200 OK carries the answer ("let's use Opus"). That's how both sides agree where the audio lane goes.RFC 3261
  • 183 Session Progress is 180's cousin: it means "early media" — you hear a real announcement before answer ("the number you dialled is disconnected"). Same lane, just audio arriving early.

🔌 Where this lives in our code. Caller identity is just another header line inside the INVITE. In the old Telnyx-direct softphone, "set the caller ID" literally meant browser JS adding a P-Asserted-Identity line to the outbound INVITE (in user_agent.js) — a hack Parrot has since retired by moving identity server-side, onto the Wazo user's configuration. The mechanism is the same message you just learned; only the author of the header changed. Lesson 4 tells that whole story, and why the move mattered for security.

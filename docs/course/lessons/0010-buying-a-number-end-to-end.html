Buying a number, end to end

Click Pay, go home — the shop, the paperwork office and five robots finish the purchase without you.

⏱ ~12 minutes🎯 Goal: trace a purchase through both databases and know every state on the way🧩 Builds on: Lessons 7, 9

The shop window

Imagine a shop that sells phone numbers. Its window is honest: it only shows shelves that actually have stock for your country — no French mobile shelf if France doesn't sell us mobiles. Some purchases need paperwork ("show us who you are first"), and the paperwork office keeps your file so you fill it in once, not once per number. And when you pay, you don't wait at the counter: your receipt just says ordering, you go home, and the goods arrive by doorbell — the webhook you met in Lesson 9. The window is coverage, the office is requirements, the receipt is a state machine, the doorbell is a signed phone_number.status event. Here is the trip, drawn.

Big idea: Pay doesn't buy anything. Clicking Pay writes local ordering rows and returns. The money hold, the Telnyx order and the activation all happen later, in broker crons — and the truth comes back by doorbell, never by the wizard waiting on a carrier.

The window only shows stocked shelves (coverage)

When the wizard opens, it needs to know which countries sell which number types. Naive design: ask the shop per country, per open. Actual design: get_all_country_coverage() fetches Telnyx's entire coverage table through the shop in one call, and the wizard caches it — with a trick worth stealing:

@api.ormcache("day", "is_demo")
def _get_coverage_map(self, day, is_demo):
    return get_phone_service_api(self.env).get_all_country_coverage()

Both arguments exist only to key the cache. day is fields.Date.today(): tomorrow the key changes, so yesterday's entry is simply never hit again — a daily-TTL cache with zero invalidation code. is_demo keeps the demo and real maps apart so toggling test mode never serves the wrong one.§5

That map drives the whole form. The country dropdown is domain-filtered to covered countries (country_id_domain); the type dropdown is a filterable_selection widget whitelisted by available_did_number_type_codes. The sellable types are DID_NUMBER_TYPES = [mobile, local, national, toll_free] — no shared_cost, no "all types" option — and the default is the first covered type in that canonical order (usually Mobile).

Degradation is deliberate, and asymmetric. Shop unreachable → offer the full sellable list and log a warning: never block a customer over a failed cache fetch. Country genuinely covers nothing → hide the type field, show an alert, disable Search: never let them search shelves that don't exist.

The paperwork office (requirements, briefly)

You met the four flavours already: textual (type a VAT number), document (upload a scan), address (prove local presence), action (do an identity check on the carrier's site). What matters for the purchase flow is who owns which paper.§6

One group per (country, type), shared by all your DIDs of that combo. The wizard carries a single requirement_group_id plus a plain has_requirements Boolean, set by one get_requirements probe during search. Buy a second Belgian local number next year and the approved group is simply reused — no forms. Except action requirements, which are per-DID: created at purchase from the group's template, tied to did_number_id, and exactly what keeps a number stuck in pending until you complete the verification.

The shared-group warning. Because the group is shared, resubmitting its papers affects every pending order of that country+type — so the submit form warns when shared_pending_count > 1. Active numbers keep their approved papers.

One refinement over Lesson 7's version: addresses are validated at write time, field by field — format checks, then the broker's validate_address, with Telnyx's numeric error codes translated into per-field messages. Your postcode is wrong while you type it, not at submit.

Every state on the way

A number lives in two ledgers at once — your database and the shop's. The states line up almost one-to-one:§12

Enterprise voip.did.numberShop phone_service.numberMeaning
—draftprice quote cached (1 h TTL) — the customer never sees this
orderingorderingPay clicked; waiting for the shop's submit cron
pendingpendingTelnyx is working on it (or an action paper is missing)
activeactiveyours, ringing, billed monthly
suspendedsuspendedcut for non-payment — reversible, still owned (and still paid for)
failurefailureorder failed for good; the credit hold is released
releasedreleasedgiven back; anyone may buy it now

Shop-side constants make the two families explicit: CHARGEABLE_STATES = (active, pending, suspended) and REORDERABLE_STATES = (draft, failure, released) — "re-orderable" means precisely "we don't currently own it on Telnyx".

The re-buy rule: a purchase can resurrect a record. There is a global UNIQUE(did_number), so re-buying a released or failed number cannot create a fresh row — it would crash at Pay. Instead _create_or_reuse_for_order flips the old row back to ordering: chatter history preserved, the previous order's per-DID action requirements wiped, and extension_id was already force-cleared when the row entered released/failure. Never assume a purchase produces fresh ids — not in tests, not in UIs.

Who guards the money

Three guards, in increasing order of authority:

1. The wizard's snapshot. iap_balance is fetched once at wizard open — a default=lambda, not a compute — with an explicit Refresh button. A compute would refire on every onchange, and each recompute is a full IAP round-trip. Snapshot + button costs one call; the price is mild staleness, which guard 3 covers anyway.

2. The prorated hold. You never prepay a full month for a number bought on the 28th. At order time the shop authorizes a prorated first month (remaining days × monthly price), then captures on Telnyx success or cancels on failure — like a hotel deposit.

3. The shop's authorize is the authoritative check. The wizard's balance gate is courtesy UX; the hold either succeeds or the order fails with a keyed, translated error — insufficient_credits comes back through the doorbell and is rendered in the DID's chatter in your language, not as raw broker text.

And the ownership doctrine, in two lines: the shop stamps customer_reference = client_id on every Telnyx order — the sole marker that a Telnyx number is ours. A daily reconcile compares the two ledgers and is tear-down only: it releases drift, it never re-provisions. The ~90-line comment atop the shop's number model is the canon.

⌨️ Where this lives in our code

voip/wizards/voip_did_number_search_wizard.py — the whole window: _get_coverage_map (the ormcache trick), _coverage_type_codes (degradation), and the _compute_coverage / _compute_did_number_type split. That split is an ORM gotcha worth one breath: if any computed field is passed to create(), Odoo skips the compute for every field sharing that method — so creating a wizard with a did_number_type would have left the whitelist empty. Two fields, two methods, both compute.

voip/models/voip_did_number.py — _create_or_reuse_for_order (the resurrection) and _handle_status_event (the doorbell handler: payload guard, state validated against the selection, write under voip_skip_pbx_sync, bus notify, then best-effort PBX sync).

iap_services/phone_service/models/phone_service_number.py (iap-apps repo) — the doctrine comment: two ledgers, customer_reference ownership, tear-down-only reconcile.
